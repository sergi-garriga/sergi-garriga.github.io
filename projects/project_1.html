project 1

The research delves into the 'nutritional transition' by analyzing documentary and archival sources, particularly focusing on meat consumption trends in Barcelona from the 18th to the 20th century.

key findings

Evolution of Consumption: It examines shifts in supply, distribution systems, and changes in meat types and quantities consumed, shedding light on the structural changes in consumption habits.

Structural Shift: Confirms a sustained structural change in meat consumption, potentially linked to factors beyond municipal policy, such as railway development and market consolidation.

Spatial Accessibility & Consumption: The research links consumers' spatial access to meat with stratified consumption patterns.

challenges

Specificity vs. Generalization: Addressing the challenges of generalizations within the concept of the 'nutritional transition,' emphasizing the necessity to consider specific historical and geographical contexts.

Attribution of Changes: Difficulties in attributing increased meat consumption solely to municipal policy changes, highlighting a multifactorial complexity.

Social Stratification: Limitations in understanding the stratification of consumption due to insufficient exhaustive social study in documentary sources had to be addressed with indicators of social strata.
